Qno3:Same masses are attached to different springs, one is vibrating faster. Why?

Two important factors do affect the period of a simple harmonic oscillator. The period is related to how stiff the system is. A very stiff object has a large force constant 'k', which causes the system to have a smaller period. For example, you can adjust a diving board's stiffness - the stiffer it is, the faster it vibrates, and the shorter its period. Period also depends on the mass of the oscillating system. The more massive the system is, the longer the period.

Qno4:Why do water waves refract at the boundary of shallow water and deep water in ripple tank experiment?

Water waves refract at the boundary of shallow and deep water because the speed of waves in water is influenced by the depth of water. When a wave travels from shallow to deep or deep to shallow water its velocity changes, this change in velocity causes refraction of the wave. * Water refracts at the boundary of shallow and deep water due to change in speed and wavelength of waves. When the speed of waves decreases their wavelength also changes. As a resuit the wave changes its direction i.e. refracted

Qno5: Under what conditions are the waves diffracted the most?

When the opening is made small (but comparable with the wavelength of waves) then the waves diffract the most. The diffraction of waves depends upon the opening of the obstacles. Waves are diffracted most strongly when the size of the opening of obstacle is less than or equal to the size of the wavelength of generated waves.
